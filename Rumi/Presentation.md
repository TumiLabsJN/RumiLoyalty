### Slide 8: "Psychology in Action"

**Header:** Psychology in Action â€” Why These Choices Work

**Example 1: Welcome Gift at Bronze (Tier 1)**

> "The moment a creator makes their first sale â€” even $5 â€” they receive a small gift card."

**Why it works:**
- **OWNERSHIP** â€” The Endowment Effect
- People value things more once they own them
- They're now *invested* in the program
- They'll return to protect and grow what they have

**Quote:** *"A creator who owns something in your program is 3x more likely to stay engaged than one who doesn't."*

---

**Example 2: Locked Reward Previews**

> "Bronze creators can SEE Gold rewards... but can't claim them yet."

**Why it works:**
- **SCARCITY** â€” The "Window Shopping" Effect
- Creates desire and aspiration
- They're not just working toward a tier â€” they're working toward THAT specific reward
- Wanting what you can't have yet

**Quote:** *"Wanting what you can't have is the oldest motivator in human psychology."*

---

**Example 3: Checkpoint Maintenance**

> "Gold creators must maintain $X in sales every 4 months or drop back to Silver."

**Why it works:**
- **AVOIDANCE** â€” Loss Aversion (Kahneman & Tversky)
- Loss aversion is **2x more powerful** than potential gain
- A creator who might slack off will push harder to KEEP their status than to earn it again
- Creates sustained engagement, not one-time achievement

**Quote:** *"The fear of losing Gold status is twice as motivating as the desire to earn it."*

---

**Example 4: Raffles**

> "Creators can enter raffles for premium prizes â€” physical gifts, experiences, large gift cards."

**Why it works:**
- **UNPREDICTABILITY** â€” Variable Rewards
- The same psychology that makes slot machines engaging
- Even if odds are low, the *possibility* of winning creates excitement
- Keeps creators checking back: "Did I win?"

**Quote:** *"Unpredictability transforms routine into excitement."*
